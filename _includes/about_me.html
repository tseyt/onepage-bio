About Me

Taner is an undergraduate researcher at Stony Brook University pursuing his Bachelor’s in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics and Statistics. In his work, he seeks to apply Software Engineering principles to architect better data and governance models within applications. Taner specializes in and is passionate about using blockchain and distributed ledger technologies in his work and research to find ways to better secure users’ rights and property.

  • Presented research on the benefits of health-tracking wearables to AIG, a Fortune 500 Insurance company.
  • Researching and creating proof-of-concept of a bridge between EOSIO and ETH blockchains to improve speed and reduce development hours.
